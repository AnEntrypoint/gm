agentplug daemon.rs per-project concurrency evolution: first fix moved from one fully-sequential dispatch loop over all registered roots (one slow exec_js on project A froze every other project's heartbeat and starved their dispatches, driving callers to time out and spawn competing standalone watchers) to thread::scope-based chunking, MAX_CONCURRENT_PROJECTS(4)-at-a-time. That chunking itself had a second bug: `known_roots.chunks(N)` was a SEQUENTIAL outer loop over fixed chunks, so chunk 2 could not start until chunk 1's thread::scope fully joined -- on a 74+ project registry, one slow dispatch in chunk 1 starved every root in every LATER chunk too, not just its 3 chunk-mates. Live-witnessed: registered a 74th project, fired a 15-20s busy exec_js against the 1st-registered (chunk 1) concurrently with a trivial health dispatch against the 74th (chunk 19) -- health took 25-31 SECONDS instead of near-instant. Fixed to a genuine bounded-concurrency work-stealing pool: pull every root's ProjectPlugins up front into a Mutex<Vec>, spawn worker_count=min(max_concurrent_projects, len) threads each looping 'pop next root, dispatch it, repeat' until the queue is empty -- a slow root now occupies exactly one of the 4 worker slots, the other 3 keep pulling fresh roots instead of idling at a chunk boundary. Ownership-safe with no unsafe: each (root, ProjectPlugins) pair is MOVED into the shared queue so a worker that pops one holds exclusive ownership, no aliasing.
