AUDIT-MO invariant (two layers, not one): gmsniff + ccsniff exist to OUTPUT USABLE HISTORY, not only to flag problems. The heuristic scans (gmsniff --list-deviations, ccsniff --*-discipline) are the FLOOR -- they catch the KNOWN problem-classes. The MANUAL history review is the CEILING -- read the actual record (gmsniff --list-sessions to eyeball the fleet's phase-walk/disp/prd/dev counts, then gmsniff --tree <sess> for a session's chronological story of phases/dispatches/recalls/reasons; ccsniff raw transcript + --list-tools) hunting for novel problems no heuristic class anticipated. A session that looks off to the COUNT (high dispatches, low prd-resolved, no deviation) is judged by READING its --tree, not the count -- e.g. a session showing repeated dispatch.end verb=browser + browser.witness-marked is healthy live-page witness debugging, not spinning. Every audit fire runs BOTH layers: the heuristic scans AND a manual history pass over the recent sessions; convergence is not 'deviations=0', it is 'deviations=0 AND the manual history read shows nothing novel-wrong'.
